Parkinson's Disease is currently diagnosed with a subjective test by a trained neurologist.

  • Misdiagnosis rates are high. Studies range from 9-34%3,4,5,6
  • Diagnosis is difficult in early stages of the disease as symptoms have not fully manifested 3
  • There are a number of diseases with similar symptoms:  7
    • Essential Tremor
    • Dystonia
    • Multiple Systems Atrophy
    • Supranuclear Palsy
    • Huntingtons Disease and more

Prior Work

Current literature proposes two main applications: Diagnosis and Monitoring

Diagnosis

  • Results are promising, with many papers reporting greater than 90% classification accuracy.
    • The basis of these results are flawed. All current datasets consist of (often late stage) patients already diagnosed with PD.

Monitoring

  • PD progression is generally tracked with a scale, the most common being MDS-UPDRS 8
    • Evaluation of progression currently requires visits to a specialist
    • Machine Learning provides opportunities for low cost at-home diagnosis

Applications of Machine Learning in Parkinson's Diagnosis

  • Without a standardised longitudinal dataset, results from ML models are meaningless
  • The fundamental question:
    Can Machine Learning diagnose PD better than a trained neurologist?

Current Datasets

  • Current research is primarily based on two public datasets:
    • Little et al. (2009)26 23 PD and 8 control; pre-processed features
    • Sankar et al. (2013)27 20 PD and 20 control; raw recordings
  • The small size of both datasets limits the usefulness of feature evaluation
  • All patients in both datasets exhibit speech difficulties

The mPower Dataset 36

65,000 speech recordings from 6,000 patients, 1,400 with Parkinson's Disease
Of the 1,300 PD patients with UPDRS data, 250 have noted no speech deficit.

“A ML model is only as good as its features.”

Limitations and Future Work

The mPower dataset lacks quality control.
  • Preprocessing is a neccessary step to filter undesirable recordings
  • Audio files are filtered and ranked based on hand-weighted factors, with the best selected for feature extraction
  • Optimally, a Machine Learning backed model can be used to automatically filter audio and all recordings considered as part of an ensemble model.

Limitations and Shortcomings

  • The UPDRS questionnarie is filled out by users - responses may not reflect clinical diagnosis

Future Work (in order of importance)

  1. Investigating the use of a time-series model such as a HMM or RNN to train and predict on short time features (e.g, raw glottal cycles)
  2. Experiment on motor features
